About me

I build machines that do things in the real world.

I’m a robotics engineer and systems thinker focused on the point where software meets hardware at scale. I care about systems that don’t just analyze reality, but interact with it: machines that sense, decide, and act.

My work spans robotics, controls, manufacturing systems, and automation. I like solving problems where the output is not just a model or a report, but a better system: cleaner logic, better feedback, fewer failures, tighter control, and more intelligent behavior.

I think in first principles: what is the system optimizing for, where is the bottleneck, and what happens if we remove it? That’s the lens I bring to factory lines, robotic platforms, and infrastructure that touches reality.

Long term, I want to build high-leverage robotic systems— machines that extend human capability, reduce friction, and make the physical world more intelligent.
